One Direction

One Direction, often shortened to 1D, is an English-Irish pop boy band formed in London, England in 2010. The group is composed of Niall Horan, Liam Payne, Harry Styles, Louis Tomlinson, and Zayn Malik. In March 2015, Zayn Malik left the band. A couple of months later and after releasing their final album as a group, they decided to go on an 18-month hiatus. The world is waiting for their 18-month hiatus to finish.

TRUE DAMAGE

True Damage is a virtual band in real-life comprised of League of Legends champions which are performed by musicians such as Duckwrth (Ekko), Soyeon (Akali), Thutmose (Ekko), Becky G (Qiyana), and Keke Palmer (Senna). Each member of the band has a skin corresponding to their True Damage alter-ego, which is styled after modern pop music. They were formed by Akali in 2019 after the global success of K/DA.

Why Don’t We

Why Don’t We, shortened to WDW, is an American boy band consisting of Jonah Marais, Corbyn Besson, Daniel Seavey, Jack Avery, and Zach Herron. They were formed in 2016 and have released two studio albums and six extended plays. The band has cited Justin Bieber as their main musical inspiration, in addition to 5 Seconds of Summer, Boyz II Men, The Beatles, Drake, Ed Sheeran, Childish Gambino, Jon Bellion, Post Malone, Frank Ocean and CNCO.

K/DA

K/DA is a virtual K-pop girl group consisting of four themed versions of League of Legends characters: Ahri, Akali, Evelynn, and Kai’Sa. They were mainly voiced by real-life musicians such as Miyeon, Soyeon, Madison Beer, and Jaira Burns respectively. They debuted in 2018 and went on a break for two years until they made a comeback in 2020. Their EP that was released on their comeback featured more real-life artists namely TWICE, Bea Miller, Wolftyla, Lexie Liu, Kim Petras, Bekuh Boom, Aluna, and Annika Wells.

Justin Bieber

Justin Drew Bieber is a Canadian singer. Bieber is widely recognized for his genre-melding musicianship and has played an influential role in modern-day popular music. He was discovered by American record executive Scooter Braun and signed with RBMG Records in 2008, gaining recognition with the release of his debut seven-track EP My World (2009) and soon establishing himself as a teen idol.

Ariana Grande

Ariana Grande-Butera, known professionally as Ariana Grande, is an American actress and singer known for playing Cat Valentine in the Nickelodeon TV series Victorious and its spin-off Sam & Cat. Her four-octave vocal range has received critical acclaim, and her personal life has been the subject of widespread media attention. She has received numerous accolades throughout her career, including two Grammy Awards, one Brit Award, one Bambi Award, two Billboard Music Awards, three American Music Awards, nine MTV Video Music Awards, and 27 Guinness World Records.

LANY

LANY is an American pop rock band from Los Angeles. Formed in Nashville in 2014, the band consists of Paul Jason Klein and Jake Clifford Goss. Signed to Polydor and Interscope Records, the band have released three top 40 albums: LANY, Malibu Nights, and Mama’s Boy.

keshi

Casey Luong, known mononymously by his stage name Keshi, is an American singer, songwriter, record producer, and multi-instrumentalist. Known for his distant falsetto vocals and textural instrumentals, he has accumulated over one billion streams with songs such as “Like I Need U”, “2 Soon” and “Right Here”.

Ne-Yo

Shaffer Chimere Smith, known professionally as Ne-Yo, is an American singer, songwriter, actor, dancer, and record producer. He gained fame for his songwriting abilities when he penned Mario’s 2004 hit “Let Me Love You”. The single’s successful release prompted a meeting between Ne-Yo and Def Jam’s then-president Jay-Z, resulting in a long-tenured recording contract.

Seraphine

Seraphine is a League of Legends character that is also a musician (voiced by Jasmine Clarke) in real-life. She started doing covers in 2020 with the songs Childhood Dreams, All The Things She Said, and POP/STARS. After K/DA discovered her cover of their song, they were thrilled and asked Seraphine to collaborate on their upcoming EP. She collaborated on one song titled MORE (which in this song was voiced by Lexie Liu instead of Jasmine Clarke). She also has original songs titled Made Me This Way and Starry-Eyed Songstress.

Bruno Mars

Peter Gene Hernandez, known professionally as Bruno Mars, is an American singer-songwriter, record producer, musician, dancer, and music video director. He is known for his stage performances, retro showmanship, and for performing in a wide range of musical styles, including pop, R&B, funk, soul, reggae, disco, and rock. Mars is accompanied by his band, the Hooligans, who play a variety of instruments, such as electric guitar, bass, piano, keyboards, drums, and horns, and also serve as backup singers and dancers.

Charlie Puth

Charles Otto Puth Jr. is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. His initial exposure came through the viral success of his song videos uploaded to YouTube. Atlantic Records and Artist Partner Group eventually sought the artist and released his debut single, “Marvin Gaye” (featuring Meghan Trainor), in 2015. His next single and first feature, “See You Again”, which he co-wrote, co-produced, and performed with Atlantic label-mate Wiz Khalifa for the Furious 7 soundtrack as a tribute to actor Paul Walker. It peaked at number one on the US Billboard Hot 100 for 12 non-consecutive weeks. After the success of “See You Again”, he gained worldwide recognition for multiple subsequent releases, including his next single, “One Call Away”. The single reached number 12 on the Billboard Hot 100.

The Weeknd

Abel Makkonen Tesfaye, known professionally as the Weeknd, is a Canadian singer, songwriter, and record producer. Noted for his distinctive singing voice with a soaring falsetto and its singular tremolo. He has received numerous accolades throughout his career, including 3 Grammy Awards, 6 American Music Awards, 19 Billboard Music Awards, 2 MTV Video Music Awards, 15 Juno Awards, and an Academy Award nomination.

John Roa

John Neil Torrente Roa (born January 25, 2000) simply John Roa, formerly known as JRoa, is a Filipino singer-songwriter under Viva Records. He was a former member of hip-hop boy group Ex Battalion. He made his solo debut on April 4, 2017 with his second digital single “Taguan”.
